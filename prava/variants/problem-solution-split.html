The Transformation

Stop Managing Permissions · Start Sharing With People

Before

The Assignment

Email client needed file sharing with "proper" access control. The spec called for technical permission management.

What Was Wrong

  • Technical jargon (A, R, W, M, D permissions)
  • Complex checkboxes and matrices
  • Focused on "rights management"
  • Required understanding access control
  • Didn't match user mental model
  • Made simple task feel complicated

Users don't want to "manage permissions". They want to share with Anna.

After

The Solution

Years of iteration led to this: Type a name, click, share. No permission matrices. No technical jargon.

What Changed

  • Search finds people instantly
  • Cards show recommended contacts
  • Pills display selections clearly
  • Human language ("Who can edit?")
  • Smart defaults (most common case)
  • Progressive disclosure (advanced hidden)

Result: People get to their goal without thinking about "permissions".

How Did We Get Here?

Over the years, dozens of paper sketches explored different approaches. Each iteration moved closer to matching the user's mental model: thinking in terms of people, not permissions.
