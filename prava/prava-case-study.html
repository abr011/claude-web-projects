The Assignment

Build a Rights Management System

The original request: Create an interface for managing document permissions. Users need to control who can Read, Write, and Delete files.

Initial requirements from IceWarp

Traditional folder permissions dialog - complex and intimidating

Finding the Real Problem

It's Not About Managing Rights

Through observation and questioning, the real problem emerged:

"I just want to quickly share this document with Anna and Petr. Why is this so hard?"

Users don't think in terms of "permissions" or "access control lists". They think about people and actions.

Translation

From Technical Jargon to Human Language

RWD Permissions
Read, Write, Delete access control
→
Who can edit?
Who can add someone else?

Instead of managing rights, we focused on answering two simple questions:

  • Who are the people I want to share with?
  • What can they do?

The Solution

Sharing Without Complexity

We removed everything that stood between the user and their goal: quickly finding and sharing with the right people.

Type a name, click, share. That's it.

What We Did

Removed, Not Added

  • Removed the concept of "permissions management"
  • Removed technical jargon (RWD → edit/add)
  • Removed complex dialogs and checkboxes
  • Removed the need to understand access control
  • Added search and autocomplete
  • Added visual feedback (pills, highlights)
  • Added smart defaults
Finding what to remove is more valuable than deciding what to add.
